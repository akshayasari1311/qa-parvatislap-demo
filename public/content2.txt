Why Choose Parvati's Lap Hostel in Parvati Valley
Parvati's Lap Hostel is a peaceful mountain stay located in Lapas Village near Kasol in the stunning Parvati Valley of Himachal Pradesh. Surrounded by pine forests, Himalayan peaks, and scenic trekking trails, the hostel offers an authentic backpacker and trekking experience in the heart of the Himalayas. Its unique location makes it an ideal stay for travelers exploring Kasol, Tosh, Chalal, Grahan Village, and the famous Kheerganga Trek.

Designed especially for backpackers, trekkers, solo travelers, and adventure seekers, Parvati's Lap combines natural beauty with comfort and affordability. Guests can stay in eco-inspired wooden dormitories, cozy attic rooms, balcony rooms with breathtaking mountain views, or spacious group dorms that are perfect for trekking teams and backpacker communities. The architecture reflects traditional Himachali design using wood and stone, giving visitors a true Himalayan mountain living experience.

One of the biggest advantages of staying at Parvati's Lap is its proximity to some of the most popular trekking routes in Parvati Valley. Trekkers frequently choose the hostel as a base for adventures like the Kheerganga Trek, Grahan Village Trek, Rasol Trek, Chalal Trek, Jiwa Nala Trek, and Sargi Trek. For more experienced hikers, routes like the Sar Pass Trek and Bhandak Thach Trek provide unforgettable high-altitude Himalayan experiences.

After a day of exploring the mountains, guests can relax at our Adhikara Cafe, enjoy warm meals, sip tea with panoramic valley views, or socialize with fellow travelers from around the world. The hostel's peaceful atmosphere, clean modern facilities, and friendly backpacker community create a welcoming space for travelers looking to connect, unwind, and experience the true spirit of Parvati Valley.

If you're searching for a budget hostel near Kasol with mountain views, trekking access, and a vibrant backpacker community, Parvati's Lap Hostel offers the perfect base to begin your Himalayan adventure in Parvati Valley.
